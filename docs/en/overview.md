# Product overview

English · [Русский](../overview.md)

**TerraRun** is a mobile ecosystem for runners where the map becomes a playing field: users capture territories with real routes, leave run traces, compete in clubs, and complete the “Traveler” meta-quest based on accumulated distance.

**Website:** [terrarun.ru](https://terrarun.ru)

---

## Problem and opportunity

| Market pain | How TerraRun responds |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Running apps are strong on stats but weak on emotion and retention | Game map: territories, difficulty layers, zone conflicts, achievements |
| Communities live on Telegram and Instagram, not in the product | Clubs, feed, chats, events, and trail flags — inside the app |
| Brands and organizers struggle to measure offline activity | Geo-linked activities, events, POI bonuses, club analytics |
| Coaches lack a digital storefront next to the map | Trainer catalog, geotags, schedule, service packages |

**Target market:** Russia and CIS — a growing amateur running segment, parkrun culture, club races, corporate and city initiatives. TerraRun fills the **location-based fitness** niche with a social layer.

## Competitive positioning

| | Strava / Nike Run Club | TerraRun |
|---|------------------------|----------|
| Focus | Stats and social | **Map + game + community** |
| Territories / AR game | No | **Yes, with server-side zone conflicts** |
| Clubs and events | Limited | **Built into the product** |
| Distance meta-quest | No | **World Journey** |
| AI coach | Limited / foreign | **Local stack** |
| Platform | Closed ecosystem | **API + partner integrations** |

TerraRun doesn’t replace Strava — it **adds a game and location layer** that category leaders lack.

## Why now

1. **Location-based fitness** — a growing trend; running is a natural activity for this model.
2. **Open niche in Russia** — no local leader with a map and territories for runners.
3. **Ready technical foundation** — not a concept, but a working app with a cloud platform.
4. **Flexible partnership model** — from a single POI to a city-wide challenge.
